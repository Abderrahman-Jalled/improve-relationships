# Improve Relationships — Product Thesis

## Problem

Most people are bad at staying in touch. Not because they don't care, but because they lack a system. The signals are everywhere — a friend's birthday in a calendar, a colleague's promotion on LinkedIn, a long silence that crept up unnoticed — but nobody synthesizes them into action.

The result: relationships decay. People you genuinely care about hear from you less and less, until the gap feels too wide to bridge naturally.

## Why personal CRM fails

Personal CRMs share a fatal design assumption: **the user will do the work.**

- Manual contact entry. Manual tagging. Manual note-taking.
- Birthday reminders with no context behind them.
- No understanding of *why now* — just calendared nudges.
- No separation between congratulating a colleague and texting a friend for Eid.
- No tone awareness. No cultural awareness. No language awareness.

A personal CRM is a database with reminders. It doesn't detect, score, rank, or draft.

## Why OpenClaw

1. **Context access**: Calendar events, conversations, shared browser tabs, contact notes.
2. **Proactive capabilities**: Cron and heartbeat workflows for scheduled operation.
3. **Draft-first architecture**: OpenClaw already expects human-in-the-loop for outbound actions.
4. **Skill composability**: Browser review, messaging connectors, and other skills are available.

## Thesis

> **Improve Relationships turns weak social signals into timely, thoughtful outreach drafts you approve before sending.**

Relationships don't need a CRM. They need a detection layer that surfaces the right person at the right moment with the right tone and language — and gets out of the way once you approve.

## Target users

1. **Professionals with broad networks** — 100-500 meaningful contacts, reaching out to maybe 10% regularly.
2. **Culturally diverse individuals** — circles spanning traditions and languages.
3. **Founders and senior ICs** — relationships compound, and they want to invest systematically.

## Wedge

The weekly relationship digest. One prompt, once a week, surfaces 5 people you should reach out to with clear reasons and draft messages. Activation energy near zero: read, approve, done.

## What makes it 10x better

You go from "I should probably reach out to someone" to "here are 5 people, here's why, here are drafts" in one prompt.
